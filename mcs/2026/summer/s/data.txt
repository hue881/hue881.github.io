HOW TO USE THIS SCRIPT

- Lines marked [DO] are teacher actions / board work / clicks.
- Lines marked [ASK] are questions to throw to the room; wait 3-5 seconds.
- Lines marked [WATCH FOR] are checks for understanding / management notes.
- Lines marked [TIER A] / [TIER B] / [TIER C] are differentiation on-ramps.
- Total target: ~110 min (Sections 2 & 3). SECTION 1 (75 min) compression
  notes are inlined and also summarized at the very bottom.

TODAY'S ONE BIG IDEA
  "A computer does EXACTLY what you tell it - no more, no less.
   That precise list of steps is an ALGORITHM. And AI is just a machine
   that follows algorithms, so AI can be wrong - YOU are the boss."

LEARNING OBJECTIVES (from syllabus)

  - Practice responsible & safe AI/online behavior. (Obj. 8)
  - Seed the idea: AI follows instructions and can be wrong. (Obj. 8)

BLOCK 0 - SETTLE & FRAME            (0:00 - 0:02 | 2 min)
================================================================================
[DO] Greet at the door, smile, screens stay CLOSED. Today is mostly unplugged.

[SAY] "Welcome back, everyone! Yesterday we discovered something wild - that a
computer is actually kind of... not that smart. It only does EXACTLY what you
tell it. Remember the sandwich robot? Today we give that idea a real name, and
we learn how to stay safe and smart around computers and AI. Screens stay
closed for a while - this is a thinking-and-talking day."

[WATCH FOR] Set the tone: warm, low-pressure, "everyone is a beginner."

================================================================================
BLOCK 1 - WARM-UP (UNPLUGGED, NO SCREENS)   (0:02 - 0:12 | 10 min)
"What would a robot get wrong?"  +  private info
================================================================================
[DO] Post on board: "Your new robot helper asks: 'What is your home address?'"

[ASK] "Should you tell it? Turn to your neighbor - 30 seconds - say YES or NO
and WHY." 
[DO] Let pairs talk. Circulate, listen for the word "private."

[SAY] "Hands up - who said NO? ... Good. Here's the rule we'll build today:
your home address, your full name, your school, your phone number, your
passwords - those are PRIVATE. Private info is like the key to your house.
You don't hand your house key to a stranger, and you don't hand it to a robot
or a website either."

[DO] Second warm-up prompt on board: "You tell a robot: 'Go to school.'"
[ASK] "Name THREE things that could go hilariously wrong if the robot does
EXACTLY what you said and nothing more."
[DO] Take 3-4 funny answers (walks into a wall, goes to the WRONG school, walks
forever because you never said STOP, goes at 3 AM because you never said WHEN).

[SAY] "See the pattern? The robot isn't being mean - it's being LITERAL. It
did exactly what we said. The problem was OUR instructions weren't precise.
Hold onto that - it's the whole lesson."

[TIER A] Keep it concrete and physical; let them act out one "wrong robot."
[TIER C] [ASK] "What's ONE missing detail in 'go to school' that a human just
'knows' but a computer doesn't?" (Push toward: which school, when, how, stop
condition.)

[WATCH FOR] Every student says at least one thing to a neighbor. No screens yet.

================================================================================
BLOCK 2 - MINI-LESSON (PART A): INTERNET SAFETY   (0:12 - 0:20 | 8 min)
================================================================================
[DO] Reveal the "Internet Safety - Why It Matters" section on the page.

[SAY] "Five quick rules. I'll say them, you'll help me remember them later."

  1. KEEP PRIVATE INFO PRIVATE.
     [SAY] "Never share full name, address, school, phone, or passwords online.
     That info is the key to your house."

  2. STRANGERS ARE STRANGERS - even online.
     [SAY] "Online, ANYONE can pretend to be anyone. Treat an online stranger
     exactly like a stranger on the street - polite, but careful."

  3. DON'T CLICK THE TRAP.
     [SAY] "Weird links, 'YOU WON A PRIZE!' pop-ups, surprise downloads - those
     are digital traps. If it feels off, don't click. Ask an adult first."

  4. TELL A TRUSTED ADULT.
     [SAY] "If anything online makes you uncomfortable, scared, or confused -
     tell a parent, teacher, or trusted adult. You will NEVER be in trouble for
     speaking up. Speaking up is the brave, smart move."

  5. THE INTERNET REMEMBERS.
     [SAY] "Pictures and messages can be screenshotted and shared forever.
     Think before you post."

[DO] PLAY VIDEO 1 - "Internet Safety for Kids" (~2-3 min). Watch with them.
[ASK] After: "One thing from the video that stuck with you - shout it out."

[TIER A] Spend the extra minute here on rule #4 - practice the exact words:
"I need to ask a grown-up." Have them say it together once.

================================================================================
BLOCK 3 - MINI-LESSON (PART B): WHAT IS AN ALGORITHM?  (0:20 - 0:30 | 10 min)
================================================================================
[DO] Reveal: "A precise, ordered list of steps that solves a problem = ALGORITHM"

[SAY] "Here's our big word for today: ALGORITHM. It sounds fancy, but it just
means: a precise, ORDERED list of steps that solves a problem. A recipe is an
algorithm. Tying your shoes is an algorithm. Brushing your teeth is an
algorithm. The two key words are PRECISE and ORDERED."

[ASK] "Why does ORDER matter? What if I put on my shoes BEFORE my socks?"
[DO] Laugh with them. "Same steps, wrong order, totally broken. Computers care
about order a LOT."

[DO] PLAY VIDEO 2 - "Algorithms - Step by Step" (~2-3 min).

[SAY] "So remember yesterday's sandwich robot and this morning's 'go to school'
robot? Those were ALGORITHMS that weren't precise enough. Today we'll write one
that actually works."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
*** AI MOMENT (folded in here, ~5 min) - "Good Robot / Bad Robot" ***
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[SAY] "Now the AI part - and this connects PERFECTLY. AI is a computer that
follows algorithms. That's it. It's not magic, it's not alive, it's not always
right. Three things to remember about AI - I call it Good Robot / Bad Robot:"

  GOOD ROBOT: "AI is a super-helpful TOOL. It can answer questions, draw
  pictures, help you practice."

  BAD ROBOT moment #1 - GARBAGE IN, GARBAGE OUT:
  [SAY] "AI learns from examples we give it. If those examples have mistakes,
  AI repeats the mistakes - it doesn't know any better."

  BAD ROBOT moment #2 - CONFIDENT BUT WRONG:
  [SAY] "Sometimes AI makes up facts that SOUND real - and says them super
  confidently! Sounding sure is not the same as being right."

[SAY] "So the rule is: AI is a helpful tool, NOT the final answer. A human
always checks. In this room, YOU are the boss, not the AI."

[ASK] "Quick - thumbs up or down: 'If the AI sounds really confident, it must
be right.'" (Want: thumbs DOWN.)

[TIER C] [ASK] "If AI just follows rules and examples, how is that like the
'precise steps' idea we just learned? (It's the SAME idea - AI is a giant pile
of steps and examples.)"

================================================================================
BLOCK 4 - GUIDED BUILD: THE "MORNING ROUTINE" ALGORITHM  (0:30 - 0:50 | 20 min)
================================================================================
GOAL: As a CLASS, write a "morning routine" algorithm on the board, then ACT IT
OUT literally as the robot - find the bugs live and fix them.

[DO] Title the board: "ALGORITHM: Get Ready for School." Take steps from the
class, write them in order, numbered.

[SAY] "Give me step one. Remember - I'm a robot. I'll do EXACTLY what's written
and nothing more."

[DO] Collect ~5-7 steps. Then ACT IT OUT as a literal robot. Deliberately break
the vague ones for laughs and learning:
  - If a step says "get dressed" -> [DO] mime putting clothes ON TOP of pajamas.
    [SAY] "You never told me to take off my pajamas first!"
  - If a step says "eat breakfast" -> [DO] mime eating the empty bowl.
    [SAY] "You never said put cereal IN the bowl!"
  - If steps are out of order -> [DO] do them literally out of order, break it.

[SAY] "This is called DEBUGGING - finding the broken step and fixing it. Bugs
are NORMAL. Every single coder makes them. Let's fix our steps to be more
PRECISE." 
[DO] Rewrite the buggy steps together until the "robot" runs cleanly.

[SAY] "We just did real computer science: write the algorithm, test it, find
the bug, fix it. That loop never goes away - even grown-up engineers do this
all day."

[TIER A] Let them physically be the robot or draw a step. Celebrate the silly
failures - keep it playful, never 'wrong.'
[TIER C - CHALLENGE CARD] [SAY] "Add a DECISION point to our routine: 'IF it's
raining, take an umbrella; ELSE wear a hat.' That little word IF is how a
computer makes choices - we'll see it again all summer." Add it to the board.

[WATCH FOR] Every student contributes at least one step OR one bug-fix idea.
Praise the DEBUG ("nice catch!"), not just the right answer.

[SECTION 1 NOTE] Trim this block to ~15 min: do ONE strong vague-step bug and
one fix, then move on.

================================================================================
BLOCK 5 - MOVEMENT / BRAIN BREAK   (0:50 - 0:55 | 5 min)
================================================================================
[SAY] "Stand up, stretch, shake it out! Your brain needs a rest to keep
learning. Let's do our own 'algorithm' with our bodies: clap-clap-stomp,
clap-clap-stomp - precise and in order!"
[DO] Lead 3-4 rounds. Reset focus. Let standing students stay standing if needed.

================================================================================
BLOCK 6 - OPEN BUILD + SHARE-OUT   (0:55 - 1:45 | ~50 min)
Write YOUR OWN algorithm, test it with a partner, share (opt-in)
================================================================================
[DO] Reveal the "Open Build + Share-Out" section. Hand out paper.

[SAY] "Now YOU are the algorithm writer. Pick a simple everyday task - making a
sandwich, feeding a pet, getting ready for bed, drawing a smiley face. Write the
steps so precise that a literal robot could do it WITHOUT messing up."

THREE ON-RAMPS (same task, three entry points):

[TIER A - Foundations]
  [SAY] "Draw a picture for each step first, THEN label it with one or two
  words. 3 to 5 steps is perfect. Work with your buddy."
  [DO] Pair with a buddy; offer a sentence starter strip ("First... Then...
  Next... Last...").

[TIER B - Core]
  [SAY] "Write 6 or more numbered steps in words. Make each one precise. Then
  SWAP with a partner and be the literal robot for each other - find each
  other's bugs!"

[TIER C - Accelerated / Challenge Card]
  [SAY] "Write 6+ steps AND add at least one IF/ELSE decision point. Bonus:
  add a WHILE loop - 'keep getting ready UNTIL you're done' - something that
  repeats until a condition is met."
  [DO] On board show the three tier prompts:
       IF it's raining...    /    ELSE - what happens otherwise    /
       WHILE (loop) - keep going until done

PROCESS (coach this out loud as they work):
  1. WRITE your steps (precise + ordered).
  2. SWAP with a partner.
  3. Partner is the LITERAL ROBOT - acts it out / reads it cold.
  4. Find a BUG together. Fix it. (Celebrate the bug!)

[DO] Circulate. Conference with 4-6 students. Give corrections PRIVATELY and
gently; praise effort and good debugging PUBLICLY.

[WATCH FOR]
  - Stuck student? Offer a smaller task ("just brushing teeth, 3 steps").
  - Fast finisher? Hand the challenge card (add an IF, then a WHILE).
  - Quiet pair? Drop in, model being the literal robot for 20 seconds.

VOCABULARY CHECK (~3 min inside this block)
[DO] Point to the 5 words for today (bilingual handout):
     ALGORITHM, PRECISE, STEP, DEBUG, PRIVATE (info)
[ASK - CHALLENGE] "Can anyone use ALL 5 words in ONE sentence that makes
sense?" Take 1-2 brave volunteers. Cheer loudly.

QUICK CHECK - "SAFE OR NOT SAFE?" (~2 min)
[DO] Show: "You want to sign up for a free game online. It asks for your home
address. What should you do?"
[ASK] Thumbs / call-and-response. Want: "Don't give it - ask a trusted adult."
[SAY] "Exactly. Private info stays private. You are the boss."

SHARE-OUT (last ~8-10 min of the block)
[SAY] "Time to share - this is ALWAYS optional. First, everyone, 'show your
neighbor' your algorithm. Then I'd love 1 or 2 brave volunteers to read theirs
and let me be the robot."
[DO] Default to pair-share. Take 1-2 opt-in volunteers ONLY. Never cold-call.
Be the literal robot for volunteers - make it fun, find a bug, fix it together,
applaud.

[SECTION 1 NOTE] Compress this whole block to ~25 min: write (10) + partner
debug (8) + quick vocab/safe-check (4) + neighbor share (3). Skip whole-class
volunteer demos if time is tight.

================================================================================
BLOCK 7 - WRAP-UP, BIG IDEAS & EXIT TICKET   (1:45 - 1:50 | 5 min)
================================================================================
[DO] Reveal "Today's Big Ideas."

[SAY] "Three things to take home in your brain today:"
  1. SAFETY: "Keep private info private. Think before you click. Tell a trusted
     adult if something feels wrong."
  2. ALGORITHM: "A precise, ordered list of steps that solves a problem.
     Computers do EXACTLY what you tell them."
  3. AI: "AI follows algorithms. AI can be wrong. Humans always check - YOU are
     the boss, not the AI."

EXIT TICKET (tell a neighbor before you leave):
[SAY] "Before you go, turn to a neighbor and do all three:"
  - Explain what an ALGORITHM is in your OWN words.
  - Name ONE internet safety rule.
  - Name ONE way AI can go wrong.
[WATCH FOR] Listen as you dismiss - quick informal check of who's got it.

HOMEWORK (~30 min)
[SAY] "Tonight: write a PRECISE 6-step algorithm for a daily task - brushing
teeth, feeding a pet, anything. Make it so clear a literal robot couldn't mess
it up. BONUS: add one 'IF.' Bring it tomorrow - we'll robot-test a few!"

[SAY] "Awesome work today, coders. You learned a real computer-science word,
you debugged like engineers, and you got smarter about staying safe. See you
tomorrow - we open Scratch and make a sprite MOVE!"

TEACHER REMINDERS (pin these)
================================================================================
  - Encouragement-first: praise effort & debugging publicly; correct privately.
  - Sharing is OPT-IN. Never cold-call. Pair-share is the default.
  - Bugs are NORMAL and celebrated - model that with your own "robot" failures.
  - Bilingual: confirm youngest/newest students understand directions.
  - Movement is allowed; standing students are fine.
  - Keep the through-line loud all day: PRECISE STEPS -> ALGORITHM -> AI follows
    algorithms -> AI can be wrong -> YOU are the boss.
