SUMMER 2026 AI & CODING PROGRAM  ·  Modern Chinese School
TEACHING SCRIPT  ·  SESSION 1  ·  Mon Jun 29, 2026
"Welcome, Icebreakers & What Is a Computer Thinking?"
Ages 7-13 (Grades 1-9)  ·  Mixed-tier (A/B/C)  ·  Python stdlib + Scratch
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PACING TARGET: Sections 2 & 3 = 110 min.  Section 1 = 75 min (see COMPRESSION
NOTES in each block).  Daily rhythm: Warm-up -> Mini-lesson(+AI) -> Guided build
-> Brain break -> Open build+challenge -> Share-out.
SESSION GOAL: Every student feels welcome, safe, and curious; everyone leaves
saying "a computer only does EXACTLY what you tell it." Seed of AI = instructions.
MATERIALS: Name tents, markers, "sandwich robot" prop (bread/jar/knife optional),
projector, Scratch (scratch.mit.edu), account slips, AI scavenger-hunt homework
handout (bilingual EN/中文).
================================================================================

[ 00:00 - 00:10 ]  WARM-UP  (unplugged, no screens)  ── 10 min
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[STAGE] Greet students at the door. Hand each a name tent + marker. Screens DOWN.

(SAY, warm, slow):
  "Welcome! You are now officially computer programmers and AI builders. I mean
   that - by the end of this summer you will BUILD a real project you can show
   your family. Today there is one rule: it is impossible to fail. Everyone in
   this room is a beginner, including the kid who has coded before."

[DO] Icebreaker: "Two Truths and a Robot Lie."
  - Each student says 3 things about themselves: 2 true, 1 a "robot lie" (silly/
    obviously fake). The group guesses the lie.
  - Model it yourself first: "I have a dog. I teach coding. I am secretly 4,000
    robots in a coat." Let them catch the lie - laughter lowers the stakes.

[TIER A note] Youngest kids: let them whisper to a buddy or just say ONE true
  thing. Never force a turn.
[COMPRESSION - Section 1] Keep to 5 min: only ~6 volunteers share, then move on.

(TRANSITION SAY):
  "Great - you just did something a computer CANNOT do easily: you guessed what
   was true using a hunch. Hold that thought. Let's meet the machine."


[ 00:10 - 00:30 ]  MINI-LESSON + AI MOMENT  ── 15-20 min
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC: A computer does EXACTLY what it is told - nothing more, nothing less.
       (This is the seed of every AI idea this summer.)

(SAY):
  "Here is the biggest secret in this whole class. A computer is not smart. It
   is not magic. It does EXACTLY what you tell it - and ONLY that. If you forget
   a step, it does not guess. It just... breaks. Let me prove it."

>>> AI MOMENT (the 'Are You Smarter Than a Computer?' sandwich demo) <<<
[DO] You are the robot. Students are the programmers. Ask them to give you
  step-by-step instructions to make a peanut-butter sandwich. Follow them
  LITERALLY and badly:
  - "Put peanut butter on the bread." -> Place the CLOSED jar on top of the bag
    of bread. Look up innocently. "Done!"
  - "Open the jar!" -> Try to open it by tapping it. "You didn't say HOW."
  - Keep going until they spell out: pick up jar, twist lid, pick up knife, etc.

(SAY, after the laughter):
  "See? I only did EXACTLY what you said. That is a computer. That is also AI.
   People think AI 'thinks' - but underneath, it is still just following
   instructions and patterns that humans gave it. All summer, every single day,
   we'll connect what we build to how AI really works. AI is not magic. It's
   instructions and patterns. Say it with me: 'instructions and patterns.'"

[BOARD] Write the class motto: "A computer does EXACTLY what you tell it."
[CHECK FOR UNDERSTANDING] "Thumbs up if a computer can guess what you MEANT."
  (Answer: thumbs DOWN - it can't. Celebrate the kids who got it.)

[TIER C stretch aside] To the older/curious kids quietly: "Even ChatGPT is doing
  a giant version of this - predicting the next word from patterns. We'll see how
  by August."
[COMPRESSION - Section 1] Hold to 15 min total incl. AI moment; cut the second
  failed sandwich attempt.


[ 00:30 - 00:50 ]  GUIDED BUILD  (tour + accounts, together)  ── 20 min
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GOAL: Everyone successfully opens the tools and gets a Scratch account ready.
[STAGE] Screens UP now. Go slow; wait for the slowest screen before each step.

(SAY): "Let's tour our workshop. Three tools, all free, all in the browser."
[DO - project each, 1 min each]
  1. Scratch (scratch.mit.edu) - "blocks you snap together like LEGO."
  2. Python Playground - "typing code; we get here in a few weeks."
  3. Teachable Machine - "where we'll TRAIN an AI with our own camera. Later!"

[DO - Account setup, step-by-step, in unison]:
  - "Go to scratch.mit.edu. Click Join Scratch."
  - Read each field aloud; wait. Use the class account-naming convention on the
    slip (no real last names / no personal info - tie to internet safety, S2).
  - PAIR RULE: if your neighbor is stuck, help them - that's our culture.

[TIER A] Buddy-pair every young student with a calmer partner for setup.
[TIER C challenge card] "Already have Scratch? Explore the block palette and find
  ONE block you've never used. Be ready to tell us what you think it does."
[COMPRESSION - Section 1] 20 min still fits; if accounts lag, finish setup as the
  first 5 min of the open build.

[CHECK] Walk the room. Every screen should show a logged-in Scratch editor.
  Quietly fix stragglers; praise effort out loud, correct privately.


[ 00:50 - 00:55 ]  MOVEMENT / BRAIN BREAK  ── 5 min
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[DO] "Robot vs Human" stretch game: When I say "HUMAN," move freely. When I say
  "ROBOT," you may ONLY do the exact move I program: "arms up... arms down."
  Ties back to the lesson AND lets the wigglers move. Standing encouraged.
[COMPRESSION - Section 1] Keep full 5 min - the break is non-negotiable for focus.


[ 00:55 - 01:35 ]  OPEN BUILD + CHALLENGE CARDS  ── 30-40 min
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TASK: "Meet your sprite." In Scratch, make the cat sprite SAY your name when the
  green flag is clicked. (Pure first-win; deep Scratch starts Session 3.)
[DO - minimal guided start, then release]:
  - "Drag a 'when green flag clicked' block. Snap a 'say [Hello!]' block under
    it. Change 'Hello!' to your name. Click the flag. It talked! You coded!"
[STAGE] Then circulate. Conference briefly with each student. Pair-program in
  short bursts. Keep on-screen checklist visible for focus-need students:
     [ ] Logged in   [ ] Green-flag block   [ ] Say block   [ ] It runs

CHALLENGE CARDS (fast finishers - explore, don't wait):
  - [Tier B] Make the sprite also CHANGE COSTUME or make a sound.
  - [Tier C] Make it say two different things in a row (intro to sequence -
    previews Session 3). Ask: "What happens if you swap the order?"
  - [Tier A] Just change the sprite's color or pick a new sprite you like.

AI TIE-BACK (drop while circulating):
  "Your sprite said your name because you told it to - EXACTLY. It didn't choose
   your name. Remember our motto?" (Let them answer.)
[COMPRESSION - Section 1] Shorten to 25 min; skip Tier C two-line extension,
  keep the core one-line "say my name" win for everyone.


[ 01:35 - 01:45 ]  SHARE-OUT  (opt-in, warm)  ── 10 min
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[DO] "Show your neighbor" first - everyone turns a screen to one partner. Then:
  "Two brave volunteers - who wants to show the whole class?" NEVER cold-call.
(SAY, to close): "Today you proved you can make a computer do EXACTLY what you
  say. That is the entire job. Tomorrow we learn the secret word for it: an
  ALGORITHM. And we'll talk about staying safe online and being smart about AI."
[COMPRESSION - Section 1] 5 min: neighbor-share only + one optional volunteer.


[ HOMEWORK  ·  ~30 min ]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"MY AI SCAVENGER HUNT" (bilingual handout):
  Find 3 places AI shows up at home (e.g., video recommendations, a voice
  assistant, phone autocomplete). Write ONE sentence about each.
  Bring it tomorrow for our Session 2 warm-up.
