Mini-Lesson · Concept 2 of 2

What Is an Event?

In Real Life

🔔 School bell rings → students leave class
🚦 Light turns green → cars start moving
👆 You press a key → sprite moves!

Definition

An event is a trigger — something that happens which tells your program to START running a sequence of code.

The 3 Key Scratch Events

🚩 When Green Flag Clicked ⌨️ When [key] Key Pressed 👆 When This Sprite Clicked

The yellow "hat blocks" in Scratch are your event triggers!

? Think & Discuss

Without an event, does the code ever run? What would happen to a sprite with no green flag block?

🤖 AI Moment Preview

These steps in order are exactly how an AI model is trained — one step at a time, in a sequence.

Vocabulary · 5 Words for Today

Words You'll Use Today

Sequence

Steps executed one after another, in order. Like a recipe — skip step 3 and dinner burns!

Event

A trigger that starts a script. "When _____ happens, do _____." Without it, nothing runs.

Sprite

A character or object in Scratch that you program. The cat is a sprite. You can make your own!

Script

A stack of blocks that runs together when an event fires. One sprite can have many scripts!

Stage

The background canvas where sprites live. 480×360 pixels. Your sprites act out their scripts here!

🤖 AI Moment Sequence = Recipe = Training Steps

Your Scratch Script IS Like AI Training

Your Scratch Script

1 When green flag clicked
2 Move 10 steps
3 Say "Hello!"
4 Turn 90 degrees

An AI Training Run

1 Start training (trigger)
2 Show example image
3 Adjust understanding
4 Repeat for every example

The Big Idea

AI doesn't learn all at once — it learns in steps, in a sequence, just like your script runs. Change the order of training steps and the AI learns something different… or breaks completely!

If your sprite doesn't move — check that your blocks are connected to a hat block. Disconnected blocks never run!

The Big Discovery

A sprite can have multiple scripts! Each script waits for its own event. They don't interfere — they run independently.
