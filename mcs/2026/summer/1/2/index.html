🛡️ Internet Safety — Why It Matters

5 Safety Rules Every Digital Citizen Knows

Keep Private Info Private

Never share your full name, address, school, phone number, or passwords online. That information is like a key to your house.

👤
Strangers Online ≠ Friends in Real Life

Online, anyone can pretend to be someone else. Treat online strangers exactly like strangers on the street — with caution.

🚦
Think Before You Click

Weird links, "You won!" pop-ups, unexpected downloads — these are digital traps. If something feels off, don't click. Ask an adult first.

🗣️
Tell a Trusted Adult

If something online makes you uncomfortable, scared, or confused — tell a parent, teacher, or trusted adult. You will never get in trouble for speaking up.

♾️
Once Online, Always Online

Pictures and messages you post can be shared, screenshotted, and remembered forever. Think before you post!

What does this have to do with AI? AI tools you use online follow the same rules — your info stays private!

🧩 What is an Algorithm?

An algorithm is a recipe for a computer

A Recipe Analogy
  • A recipe tells you exactly what to do, step by step, in order.
  • If you skip a step, the food turns out wrong.
  • An algorithm does the same — but for a computer.
Algorithm Must-Haves
1
Precise — no guessing, no "you know what I mean"
2
In the right order — sequence matters!
3
Has a start and an end — it stops when done
Real-World Algorithms
🚦
Traffic lights — follow rules for when to turn red/green
📱
Your phone's screen lock — checks your face or passcode
🎵
Spotify/YouTube — decides what song to play next
🤖
AI — uses algorithms to decide what to say or show

A precise, ordered list of steps that solves a problem

= ALGORITHM

🤖 AI Moment · Good Robot / Bad Robot

Not all robot behavior is created equal…

Good Robot
  • ✅ Refuses to share your private information
  • ✅ Tells you when it isn't sure about something
  • ✅ Suggests you check with a trusted adult
Bad Robot
  • ❌ Asks for your home address or passwords
  • ❌ Gives you wrong information confidently
  • ❌ Acts like it's always right — no need to check
⚠️ AI Can Be Wrong!
📚
AI learns from data

If training data has mistakes, AI repeats them.

🎭
AI can "hallucinate"

It makes up facts that sound real but aren't — and says them confidently!

🧑‍⚖️
Humans must check

AI is a helpful tool, not the final answer.

If AI told you the capital of France was London — how would you check? What sources would you use?
🤔 Key question: An AI helper always gives you an answer immediately. Does that mean it's always correct?

📊 Three Tiers · Same Concept, Three On-Ramps

Everyone writes an algorithm — at their own level

Tier A — Foundations
Grades 1–3 / no experience
  • Draw your morning routine as pictures first, then add words.
  • Write at least 4 steps. Use a buddy to check.
  • Extra time on internet safety: "asking a grown-up."
Tier B — Core
Grades 3–5, some Scratch
  • Write a precise 6-step morning routine algorithm.
  • Act it out — find your bugs and debug!
  • Homework: write a new algorithm for a different daily task.
Tier C — Accelerated
Grades 6–9 / Python-curious
  • Write an algorithm with a decision point: if it's raining…
  • Add an else — what happens if it's not raining?
  • Challenge: add a while loop — keep getting ready until done!
Challenge cards let fast finishers extend instead of wait — so nobody is bored and nobody is lost.
