Big Idea · Computer Thinking

Computers don't think like us — they follow steps! 🪜

  • A computer is not magic and it is not alive. It is a very fast rule-follower.
  • It does exactly what we tell it — nothing more, nothing less.
  • "Computer thinking" means breaking a big job into tiny, clear steps a machine can follow.
  • Even the smartest AI still follows steps and patterns we set up. We'll see that all summer!

🤔 If a computer only does exactly what it's told, who has to be the careful, clear thinker — the computer, or you? 👆

Step by Step · The Secret Rule

Why computers need every single step spelled out 🥪

"Put on your shoes."

Easy! Your brain fills in hundreds of tiny steps automatically.

What your brain auto-fills:
1. Locate shoes 2. Pick them up 3. Sit down 4. Align to foot 5. Slide in 6. Tighten lace…
Robot hears: "Put on your shoes."

It freezes! Which foot? Which shoe? Lift it how high? It needs every step spelled out.

Robot needs:
Pick up left shoe. Hold opening facing right foot. Lift right foot 30cm. Insert foot. Lower foot…

A precise, ordered list of steps a computer follows is called an

ALGORITHM 🎉

AI Moment · Are You Smarter Than a Computer?

The sandwich algorithm game 🥪🤖

How the game works
  1. You write step-by-step instructions to make a PB&J sandwich.
  2. Your teacher acts as the robot and follows your instructions exactly — no guessing, no common sense.
  3. Watch what goes wrong — that's your bug! Then fix it.
Common robot "bugs"
  • 🫙"Open the peanut butter" → Robot picks up the whole jar and shakes it
  • 🍞"Put it on the bread" → Which side? Top? With what?
  • ✂️"Cut in half" → Robot uses scissors, cuts the table, the bag…

Big lesson: AI is still just following instructions and patterns — it can't read your mind! 🧠 The smarter your instructions, the smarter the result.

Compare · "Human Steps" vs "Robot Steps"

The same task — two very different sets of instructions 🆚

How a person says it
1. Open the jar
2. Spread on bread
3. Add jelly
4. Close the sandwich

4 easy steps — our brains fill in the rest.

How a robot needs it
1. Locate jar on counter
2. Grasp lid with right hand
3. Turn lid counter-clockwise
4. Insert knife ½ inch deep
5. Spread in left-right motion
…and many more!

Every detail must be said.

💡 This is computer thinking: noticing the tiny steps we usually skip, and putting them in the right order.

Vocabulary · 4 Words for Today

Your first four computer-science words 🏆

🪜Algorithm

A precise list of steps, in order, that solves a task. A recipe is an algorithm for food!

📋Instruction

One single step in the list. Computers do each instruction exactly and in order.

🤖AI

Computer programs that spot patterns and make guesses. Still following instructions — not magic.

🐛Bug

A mistake in your steps that makes things go wrong. Bugs are normal — we fix them together!
