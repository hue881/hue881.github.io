🧩 Puzzle 1 — Translate the Scratch Script to Python (no computer needed!)

🟦 This Scratch script runs when the green flag is clicked:

when 🟩 clicked
say "Hello, World!" for 2 secs
repeat 3
say "I love coding!" say "Done!"

🐍 Write the Python version — fill in the blanks:

# Python doesn't need "when clicked" # it just runs top to bottom! print() for i in range(): print() print()

💡 The last line is not indented — it runs after the loop ends!

🤖 AI Moment — Session 8

📝 Your print() follows rules you wrote:

print("I like...") print("learning...") print("new things")

Your program follows rules you wrote. Same pattern every time!

🤖 An AI predicts the same pattern — at massive scale:

You type: "I like…"
AI predicts: "…learning new things"
It has seen billions of patterns like yours!
💡 Connection: Your print() statements follow rules you wrote. AI learned the rules from millions of examples. Same pattern idea — completely different scale!

❓ Discussion — answer in 1–2 sentences:

If an AI learned only from your writing samples, what patterns would it predict? Would it be a good AI or a bad AI for everyone?
